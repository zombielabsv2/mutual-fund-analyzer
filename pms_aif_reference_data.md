## SEBI PMS Regulations (as of 2025-2026)

- **Minimum Investment:** Rs 50 lakh (raised from Rs 25 lakh in January 2020)
- **SEBI Registration:** Mandatory for all PMS providers
- **Custodian:** Independent custodian must hold client assets
- **Industry AUM:** Rs 3.8+ lakh crore (as of 2025)
- **APMI** has requested SEBI to lower the threshold citing competition from SIFs

## AIF CATEGORY III - TOP STRATEGIES

- Minimum Investment: Rs 1 Cr (Rs 25 lakh for directors/employees/fund managers)
- Typical Fee Model: "2 and 20" (2% management fee + 20% performance fee above hurdle)
- Common hurdle rates: 8-12%
- Category III AIFs are the fastest-growing segment of India's alternatives market

## KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Fee Convergence:** Most PMS charge 2-2.5% fixed + 15-20% performance fee above 8-12% hurdle. Notable exceptions: Marcellus CCP (0% fixed option), SageOne (pure profit share), Invesco (flat 10% profit share).

2. **Best Long-Term Track Records (10+ years):** ASK Growth (2001, ~17.4%), Alchemy High Growth (2002, ~19.9%), Motilal NTDOP (2003, ~15.4%).

3. **Best Recent Performers (since ~2018-2020):** Carnelian Shift (~37.6%), SageOne SSP (~40.2%), White Oak Pioneers (~25.7%).

4. **Largest AUM PMS Houses:** ASK (~Rs 26,869 Cr), Unifi (~Rs 20,934 Cr), Invesco (~Rs 20,178 Cr), Motilal Oswal (~Rs 14,483 Cr), Carnelian (~Rs 9,667 Cr).

5. **AIF Category III:** Minimum Rs 1 Cr; typical 2%+20% fees; fastest growing segment. Long-short and quant strategies emerging strongly (Swyom, Nuvama MARS, WhiteSpace Alpha).

6. **Survivorship Bias Warning:** Published returns often reflect only surviving strategies. Underperformers may have been shut down (e.g., Avendus Absolute Return Fund wound down Jan 2025).
