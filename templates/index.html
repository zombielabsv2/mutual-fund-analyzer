How This Works

This tool analyzes Indian equity mutual funds by computing their 5-year rolling returns and ranking them by a robustness score that captures not just how high the returns are, but how consistent and reliable they have been over time.

1. Fund Universe

We analyze 104 Direct Growth equity mutual funds across 11 SEBI-defined categories:

Market Cap Based

  • Large Cap — 10 funds (top 100 companies by market cap)
  • Large & Mid Cap — 10 funds
  • Mid Cap — 10 funds (101st-250th by market cap)
  • Small Cap — 11 funds (251st onwards)

Diversified / Strategy

  • Flexi Cap — 10 funds (flexible allocation)
  • Multi Cap — 6 funds (min 25% each in large/mid/small)
  • Value / Contra — 10 funds (contrarian or value investing)
  • Focused — 10 funds (max 30 stocks)

Tax Saving & Others

  • ELSS — 12 funds (3-year lock-in, 80C deduction)
  • Sectoral / Thematic — 8 funds (tech, banking, infra, etc.)
  • Index Funds — 7 funds (passive, tracks Nifty/Sensex)
Selection criteria: Only Direct Growth plans are included (no regular plans, no IDCW/dividend). Funds are chosen from top AMCs (SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, Kotak, Mirae, Nippon, DSP, UTI, Canara Robeco, Franklin, Parag Parikh, Quant, Motilal Oswal, Tata, Aditya Birla, Edelweiss, Sundaram, PGIM, Bandhan, HSBC, Invesco, and more) to ensure diversity and avoid fund-house bias. Only funds with sufficient NAV history (≥ 5 years of data) are included in rankings.

2. Rolling Returns Calculation

For each fund, we compute the 5-year CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) at every available date in the fund's history.

5-Year Rolling CAGR
CAGR = ( NAVtoday / NAV5 years ago )1/5 − 1

This is computed for every trading day where a matching NAV exists ~5 years prior (within a 15-day window). The result is a time series of 5-year returns — not a single number, but a full picture of how the fund performed across all 5-year windows in its history.

Why rolling returns? A single-point return (e.g., "this fund gave 18% over 5 years") is misleading because it depends entirely on when you entered and exited. Rolling returns show you what the return would have been if you had invested on any date and held for 5 years. This reveals consistency, not just peak performance.

3. Statistical Metrics

From the rolling returns time series, we compute these statistics for each fund:

MetricWhat It Tells You
Average ReturnThe mean 5-year CAGR across all rolling windows. Higher = better long-term wealth creation.
Minimum ReturnThe worst 5-year return the fund ever delivered. Higher = better downside protection. A positive minimum means the fund never lost money over any 5-year period.
Maximum ReturnThe best 5-year return. Shows the upside potential.
Standard DeviationHow much the rolling returns vary. Lower = more predictable returns.
Positive Periods %What percentage of all 5-year windows gave positive returns. 100% means the fund has never delivered negative returns over any 5-year stretch.

4. Robustness Score

The rankings are sorted by a robustness score that combines multiple dimensions of performance into a single number:

Robustness Score
Score = (Avg Return × Positive%) / (1 + StdDev / 10)

This score rewards funds that have:

  • High average returns — the fund creates wealth over time
  • High positive period percentage — the fund rarely loses money over 5-year windows
  • Low standard deviation — the returns are consistent, not wildly swinging
Interpretation: A fund with 15% average return but wild swings (high std dev) and occasional negative 5-year periods will score lower than a fund with 13% average return that is rock-solid consistent. The score captures reliability of wealth creation, not just peak performance.

5. Data Source

All NAV data comes from mfapi.in, which provides historical NAV data for all AMFI-registered Indian mutual funds. The data is updated daily and goes back to each fund's inception date.

Fund categorization uses the SEBI mutual fund categorization framework (October 2017), which standardized fund types across all AMCs in India.

6. Limitations

  • Survivorship bias — Only currently active funds are analyzed. Funds that were merged or closed (likely poor performers) are excluded.
  • Past performance — Rolling returns are backward-looking. A high robustness score does not guarantee future returns.
  • Fund manager changes — A fund's track record may reflect a previous manager's skill, not the current one's.
  • AUM & liquidity — This analysis does not account for fund size, which can impact future performance (especially for small cap funds).
  • Expense ratios — NAV data is net of expenses, so the analysis implicitly accounts for costs. But Direct plans have lower costs than Regular plans.
